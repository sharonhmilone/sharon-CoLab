System 01

The Content Engine

From Prototype to Revenue Infrastructure

I designed this system to solve a specific problem: how do you produce high volumes of quality curated content for a 240K subscriber brand without burning out your editorial team on search-intent production?

The original pipeline was five steps — UpContent for article discovery, LLM-powered prompting to analyse source content, identify editorial gaps and generate new angles, Airtable for tracking, and Google Docs for final editorial review. Built on Zapier. Live in production.

That prototype proved the model. With the right source material and a well-defined editorial brief on the front end, AI handles volume without sacrificing quality. It freed the team entirely to focus on original thought leadership and contributor programming.

With internal dev support, the system has since evolved into infrastructure powering content discovery for QZ.com — Redbrick's newly acquired media network. Content discovery now runs through News Catcher, pulling signals from thousands of credible sources. The workflow supports agent calls and has been extended to monetise content recommendations through pay-per-click affiliate. It's not just a production engine anymore — it's a revenue-generating content system.

System 02

The Editorial Operations System

Contributor to Published — Automated End-to-End

The second system I built solved a different problem: how do you manage a growing contributor pipeline across outreach, pitching, onboarding, production and payment — with a small team and no room for things to fall through the cracks?

I architected a multi-board Asana environment connecting every stage of the contributor lifecycle. From the moment a potential contributor is identified, through application, pitch review, onboarding, article production, editorial approval and payment — the system routes, notifies and tracks with minimal manual intervention.

The design was intentional: human judgment at the decision points that matter (application review, pitch approval, editorial sign-off), automation everywhere else. AI handles the first touchpoint at outreach, pitch screening and generating the content brief, reducing cognitive load while keeping editorial standards high. A learning loop a.k.a. Gold Standard Library continuously refines the AI screener's discernment based on pitches that make it to published drafts.

System 03

The Editorial Intelligence Engine

AI-Native Content Cycle — From Brief to Published

In progress

Where Systems 01 and 02 layer AI onto existing infrastructure, System 03 is built AI-first from the ground up. It's a bespoke Next.js application designed around a single editorial principle: AI initiates, humans decide and the system learns.

Content briefs are generated weekly from Claude API calls to a live editorial context document maintained in Supabase. Briefs surface in a clean dashboard for editorial review where they can be approved, rejected or refined. Approved briefs trigger the generation of a first draft. Then, drafts go through inline editorial review, with the system capturing the differential between its version and the final edit. That way, every piece of feedback becomes a learning signal for the system.

This learning loop is what I consider to be the architectural centrepiece. The quality threshold is set based on the brief-to-publish rate, and this context gets incorporated into the editorial context document to improve future prompts, ensuring the system genuinely improves from editorial judgment over time.
